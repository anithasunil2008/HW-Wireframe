Pottery:

Pottery is the ceramic material which makes up potterywares, of which major types include earthenware, stoneware and porcelain. The place where such wares are made by a potter is also called a pottery.

Pottery Creation Techniques:

Hand-built and wheel thrown represent two basic techniques for creating pottery. Within those techniques there are also different ways of forming the clay.

Hand-Built Pottery:

Hand-built pottery, the first form of pottery, allows individuals to form clay without the use of a potter's wheel. Hand-Built Techniques are pinch pot, coil pot, slab pot.

Pinch Pot:

Made by kneading the clay and pressing it into the shape of a bowl, cup, dish, or pot

Coil Pot:

The clay is made into long, round clips coiled and stacked to form other shapes.

Slab Pot:

Slabs of clay are used to form clay boxes or other geometric shapes; can be combined with other hand-built techniques to make more advanced shapes

Wheel-Thrown Pottery:

Wheel-thrown pottery uses a more difficult technique than hand-built pottery and requires the use of a potter's wheel, as well as specific knowledge about how to use the wheel, such as the need to center the clay before working it.
